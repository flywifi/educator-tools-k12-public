# tiering.md
## Differentiation Engine — Tiering
Canonical source. Tiering adjusts a task so students at different points can all engage with the
**same essential objective**. Use after UDL (`udl.md`) for remaining readiness/interest/profile
variability. Distinct from MTSS tiers (intervention) — see `protocols/` and the future
`intervention-mtss` skill.

---

## 1. What to tier (Tomlinson)
- **Content** — what students learn / the materials they use to access it.
- **Process** — how they make sense of it (activities, grouping, scaffolds).
- **Product** — how they demonstrate it (format, complexity, support).

## 2. What to tier *by*
- **Readiness** — current skill relative to the objective (most common).
- **Interest** — topic/choice hooks for engagement.
- **Learning profile** — preferences/strengths in how a student works.

## 3. A practical 3-tier readiness pattern

Keep the **objective and standard constant**; vary support and complexity.

| Tier | Support | Complexity / example (fractions, gr 3) |
|---|---|---|
| **Approaching** | more scaffolding, concrete models, worked examples, sentence frames | partition simple shapes into halves/fourths with manipulatives |
| **On-level** | grade-level task with targeted support | identify/represent fractions 1/b on shapes and number lines |
| **Beyond** | less scaffolding, abstraction, extension | compare/equivalence and justify reasoning in multiple representations |

## 4. Rules
- Every tier targets the **same objective** — tiering changes the path, not the destination
  (modifying the destination is a *modification*, see `accommodations-catalog.md`).
- Avoid permanent ability labels; tiers are task-specific and fluid.
- Record tiers used in metadata (`differentiation`). Tiering feeds **Educational Quality** (QG §27).
