# udl.md
## Differentiation Engine — Universal Design for Learning (UDL)
Canonical source. UDL is the **proactive default**: design for learner variability up front, before
reaching for individual accommodations. Pairs with `tiering.md`, `english-learners.md`,
`accommodations-catalog.md`.

UDL organizes around three principles. For each, the ecosystem offers concrete options an artifact
can include.

---

## 1. Multiple means of Engagement (the "why")
Options to recruit interest, sustain effort, and support self-regulation:
- Offer choice (topic, tools, order).
- Make relevance explicit (connect to students' lives/goals).
- Vary challenge and support; build in collaboration options.
- Include self-monitoring/reflection prompts.

## 2. Multiple means of Representation (the "what")
Options so all learners can perceive and comprehend:
- Present content in more than one format (text + visual + audio).
- Pre-teach/define vocabulary; provide background knowledge.
- Use graphic organizers, models, and worked examples.
- Make patterns and big ideas explicit.

## 3. Multiple means of Action & Expression (the "how")
Options for how students demonstrate learning:
- Allow multiple response formats (write, speak, build, draw, record).
- Provide scaffolds, sentence frames, checklists, and exemplars.
- Stage tasks; support planning and goal-setting.
- Offer assistive-tech-friendly formats.

---

## How skills apply UDL

During pipeline step 3 (Differentiation), a skill selects UDL options appropriate to the artifact
and records them in metadata (`differentiation`). UDL is checked by the **Accessibility gate**
(QG §31) and contributes to **Educational Quality** (QG §27). UDL handles *most* variability
proactively; use `tiering.md` for readiness ranges, `english-learners.md` for EL needs, and
`accommodations-catalog.md` for IEP/504 specifics.
