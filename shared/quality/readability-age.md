# readability-age.md
## Readability & Age-Appropriateness
Canonical source. Feeds the **Accessibility gate** (QG §16, §31) and **Educational Quality** (QG
§27). Two audiences matter: the **student** (materials they read/use) and the **adult** (educator/
family-facing text).

---

## 1. Student-facing text by band (targets, not hard limits)

| Band | Reading-level target | Language guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **K-2** | ~grade 0–2 | short simple sentences; high-frequency words; lots of visuals; decodable where relevant |
| **3-5** | ~grade 3–5 | introduce academic vocabulary with support; clear structure; visuals + text |
| **6-8** | ~grade 6–8 | content/academic vocabulary; complex sentences with scaffolds; more text |
| **9-12** | ~grade 9–12 | discipline-specific vocabulary; sophisticated structure; cite sources |

Keep **cognitive demand** at grade level even when lowering language load (see
`shared/differentiation/english-learners.md`). Reading level applies to *student-facing* text; the
educator-facing parts of an artifact may be denser.

## 2. Age-appropriateness
- Contexts, names, and examples appropriate and inclusive for the band (no mature/unsafe themes).
- Examples are **representative and bias-aware** (varied names, cultures, contexts) —
  `SECURITY_AND_SAFETY.md`.
- Family-facing communication: plain language, jargon defined, ~grade 6–8 readability by default,
  translation-friendly.

## 3. Quick checks
- Sentence length and word complexity match the band.
- Vocabulary is pre-taught or defined where it exceeds the band.
- Visuals support (not decorate) comprehension.
- Directions are explicit and chunked.

Record notable readability decisions in metadata (`differentiation` / `rationale`). A serious
mismatch (e.g., grade-11 text in a grade-3 lesson) is an Accessibility deficiency → Remediation.
