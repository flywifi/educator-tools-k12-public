# Lesson Plan: Understanding Unit Fractions (1/b)

> Gold-standard reference output. Evaluated by quality-review → **Approved (composite 4.34)**; see
> `skills/quality-review/examples/example-evaluation.md`.

- **Subject / Grade band:** Mathematics / 3-5 (Grade 3)
- **Duration:** 45 minutes
- **Standard:** `CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.3.NF.A.1` — "Understand a fraction 1/b as the quantity formed by
  1 part when a whole is partitioned into b equal parts; understand a fraction a/b as the quantity
  formed by a parts of size 1/b." (CCSS-Math, 2010). Practice: `MP6` Attend to precision.
- **Objective:** Students will be able to partition a whole into b equal parts and name one part as
  the unit fraction 1/b, demonstrated by correctly labeling and explaining 4 of 5 exit-ticket items.

## Materials
- Fraction circles/tiles (concrete); pre-cut paper strips; whiteboards & markers; the guided-notes
  half-sheet; the exit ticket.

## Lesson sequence (gradual release)
| Phase | Time | Teacher | Students |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opening / hook | 5 | Share a "fair share" story: 1 granola bar split equally among 4 students. "How much does each get? How do we name it?" State objective. | Discuss; predict the fair share. |
| I do (model) | 10 | Fold a paper strip into 4 equal parts; shade one. Think aloud: "4 equal parts → each is 1 *fourth* → 1/4. The bottom number tells how many equal parts." Model precise language (MP6). | Watch; annotate guided notes. |
| We do (guided) | 12 | With fraction tiles, partition wholes into 2, 3, 6, 8 equal parts; name the unit fraction together; stress *equal* parts vs. unequal. | Build, name, and write 1/b with the teacher; whiteboard checks. |
| You do (independent) | 13 | Monitor; pull an approaching-level small group to the table. | Partition and label unit fractions on the practice page at their tier. |
| Closing | 5 | "Why must the parts be equal? What does the denominator tell us?" Restate objective. | Quick pair-share; respond on whiteboards. |

## Checks for understanding
- **During:** whiteboard "show me 1/3" after We-do; listen for "equal parts" language.
- **End:** exit ticket (below).
- **If students struggle:** return to concrete tiles 1:1; compare an *equal* vs *unequal* partition
  side by side; Tier 2 small group next day.

## Differentiation
- **UDL:** Engagement — the fair-share story + choice of manipulative. Representation — concrete
  (tiles) → representational (shaded models) → abstract (1/b symbol). Action/Expression — show
  understanding by building, drawing, or writing.
- **Tiering (same objective):** *Approaching* — halves/fourths with tiles + sentence frame "___
  equal parts, each part is 1/___." *On-level* — partition 2/3/6/8 with models. *Beyond* — partition,
  then explain why 1/8 < 1/4 using a drawing.
- **English learners:** pre-teach *partition, equal, whole, denominator, unit fraction*; cognate
  *fracción*; sentence frame above; pair talk before whole-group.
- **IEP/504 accommodations (match the student's actual plan):** extended time on the practice page;
  reduced item count; manipulatives available; read-aloud of directions.

## Exit ticket
**Objective measured:** name the unit fraction for an equally-partitioned whole. **Standard:** 3.NF.A.1.
1. A whole is split into 3 equal parts. What fraction names one part? **(1/3)**
2. Shade 1/4 of the rectangle [4 equal parts shown]. **(any one of the 4 parts)**
3. True/False: these parts are equal, so each is 1/2 [picture shows *unequal* halves]. **(False — parts are not equal)**
4. The denominator in 1/6 tells us ____. **(the whole is split into 6 equal parts)**
5. Draw a whole partitioned into 8 equal parts and label one part. **(1/8 labeled)**

**Using the data:** 4-5 correct → continue to a/b next lesson. 3 → light scaffold. ≤2 → Tier 2 small
group with concrete tiles.

## Assumptions
- Time = 45 min, whole-class setting (not stated in the request).
- Students have prior experience with "equal groups" from Grade 2.
