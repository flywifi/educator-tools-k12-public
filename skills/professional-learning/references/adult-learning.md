# adult-learning.md
## Adult-learning principles
What makes professional learning effective for educators (feeds Educational Quality + the coaching
tone the Safety/Professional gates expect).

## 1. Relevance & autonomy
Adults engage when learning connects to their real work and they have choice. Start from a problem of
practice the educator actually has; offer options in how they engage.

## 2. Build on experience
Treat educators as experts in their context. Surface and connect to what they already do; coaching is
*with*, not *to*.

## 3. Active practice over telling
Model the practice, let educators try it, give feedback. A PD that's all slides/lecture rarely
changes practice — build in approximation and rehearsal.

## 4. Reflection & goal ownership
Use reflective questions so the educator draws conclusions and owns the next step. Goals the teacher
sets are more durable than goals assigned to them.

## 5. Non-evaluative, asset-based language
Coaching is growth-oriented and confidential, separate from evaluation. Describe **observable**
practice ("students restated the objective in their words") rather than judgments ("good lesson").
Lead with strengths (glow) before growth.

## 6. Transfer & follow-up
One-shot PD fades. Plan for follow-up: a small commitment, a check-in, and a way to share back.
