# lesson-design.md
## Lesson design principles
What makes a lesson *instructionally effective* (feeds the Educational Quality gate, QG §27), not
just correct. Apply these during Generation.

---

## 1. Start from a measurable objective
State what students will know/do and how it will be observed. Prefer observable verbs (identify,
partition, compare, model, justify) over vague ones (understand, know). The objective must match the
**grade + intent** of the cited standard.

## 2. Gradual release ("I do / we do / you do")
- **Opening / hook** — activate prior knowledge; make relevance explicit; state the objective.
- **I do** — model the thinking explicitly (worked example).
- **We do** — guided practice with feedback.
- **You do** — independent practice at the target level.
- **Closing** — synthesize; restate the objective; quick check.

## 3. Build understanding concretely → abstractly
Especially K-8 math/science: move concrete (manipulatives) → representational (models/diagrams) →
abstract (symbols). Name which representation each step uses.

## 4. Check for understanding throughout
Plan CFUs at more than one point (not only the exit ticket): targeted questions, quick writes,
thumbs/whiteboards. Say what you'll do if students don't get it (reteach move / Tier 2 link).

## 5. Manage cognitive load
Chunk new content; provide worked examples and scaffolds; remove extraneous load (clear directions,
clean materials). Keep the *thinking* at grade level while lowering the *language/processing* barrier
for those who need it.

## 6. Differentiate by design, not as an afterthought
Bake in UDL options up front (`shared/differentiation/udl.md`); add readiness tiers
(`tiering.md`), EL scaffolds (`english-learners.md`), and IEP accommodations
(`accommodations-catalog.md`) where relevant. Tiers target the **same objective**.

## 7. Align everything
Objective ↔ instruction ↔ practice ↔ check. If the exit ticket measures something the lesson didn't
teach (or vice versa), fix the alignment before shipping (QG §26).
